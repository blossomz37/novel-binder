### 2.16 LitRPG / Progression-SF

- **Core reader expectation**: RPG-style interface (status screens, level-up notifications, skill trees, inventory) embedded in SF or fantasy world. Reader's pleasure is watching statistical progression — characters becoming quantifiably more powerful. Modern readers expect character depth alongside stat progression, meaningful consequences, better female representation, and "blue boxes that serve the story rather than pad word count." [SRC] [READER] [Chapter Chronicles](https://www.chapterchronicles.com/blog/the-land-og-litrpg-conventions/)
- **Character tropes**: (1) The ordinary person (veteran, slacker, pet owner) who gains extraordinary capability through system-assigned power; (2) The pet companion as equal or superior — Princess Donut (Carl's cat) as breakout character in *Dungeon Crawler Carl*; (3) The reluctant hero who keeps getting pulled back despite trying to avoid conflict; (4) The strategist who outplays the system's intended parameters; (5) The mentor character who is weaker but wiser, eventually transcended by protagonist
- **Plot tropes**: (1) The system apocalypse — aliens invade/restructure Earth as a game show or dungeon; humanity is the contestant (*Dungeon Crawler Carl*: alien invasion + reality TV + dungeon); (2) The floor-by-floor escalation — each chapter unit advances power and reveals new threat tier; (3) The 4th-wall-aware meta commentary — protagonist increasingly aware of the story/game structure; (4) The faction conflict — protagonist must navigate competing institutional powers while leveling; (5) The revelation that the system itself is the real villain
- **Theme tropes**: (1) The small vs. the system — corporate or alien power structures vs. the individual leveling up; (2) Trauma processed through competence — pre-apocalypse loss rehabilitated through capability; (3) Genocidal stakes treated with "sincerity from a contemporary human viewpoint" (*Dungeon Crawler Carl* addresses genocide, cannibalism, moral horror alongside humor); (4) The question of what human life is worth in a system that quantifies everything; (5) Community vs. isolation in power progression
- **Tonal range**: Dark/serious (Defiance of the Fall, Cradle/Will Wight — high stakes, some grimness); comedic/pop (*Dungeon Crawler Carl* — dark humor, self-aware absurdism); meme/wish-fulfillment (He Who Fights with Monsters, Primal Hunter — pure power-scaling)
- **Adjacent niches**: Military SF (LitRPG overlaps when protagonist is a soldier leveling up through combat); Isekai/portal fantasy (LitRPG's cousin); Hard SF (almost no overlap — LitRPG readers not interested in scientific accuracy)
- **Key divergence points**: Military SF is institution-grounded and realistic; LitRPG military variant is power-fantasy in RPG skin. LitRPG and isekai share transported-to-game-world premise; LitRPG emphasizes the stat-progression system while isekai emphasizes cultural fish-out-of-water.
- **Recent market examples**: *Dungeon Crawler Carl* series (Matt Dinniman) — Books a Million Book of the Year; movie in development; He Who Fights with Monsters (Jason Cheyne/Shirtaloon) — ~$170K/month creator revenue from Patreon (17,000 patrons); The Primal Hunter series (Zogarth) — KU dominant; 12 Miles Below (Keith Scheuer, 2025); IGN Best LitRPG 2026: He Who Fights, Path of Ascension, DCC, Defiance of the Fall
- **Demand vs. supply assessment**: Royal Road grew from 90M to 160M monthly visits in one year (2023–2024). Revenue is massive: Primal Hunter author earns ~$70K/month, He Who Fights author ~$170K/month. Trad publishers have "taken notice" and are "actively seeking writers and projects." Simon Maverick (S&S audio-first imprint 2025) directly targets this pipeline. [GAP] in adult LitRPG that crosses to trad readers.
