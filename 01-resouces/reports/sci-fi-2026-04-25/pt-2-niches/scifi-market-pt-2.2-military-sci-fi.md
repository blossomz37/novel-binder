### 2.2 Military Sci-Fi

- **Core reader expectation**: Elite squads, alien threat, chain of command, boot camp origin, last stand, black ops — these are contractual genre elements, not aspirational. [SRC] [READER] [WriteAIBook](https://writeaibook.com/tools/military-scifi-tropes-list.html)
- **Character tropes**: (1) The welfare rat turned soldier — underclass protagonist who earns capability and purpose through service (Kloos's Andrew Grayson); (2) The tactical genius with moral compass (Campbell's Black Jack Geary); (3) The skeptical veteran who sees through institutional failure; (4) The loyal squad as found family with distinct specializations; (5) The female protagonist who navigates institutional misogyny without it being her only story
- **Plot tropes**: (1) Interplanetary war with alien threat as backdrop; human political corruption as proximate driver; (2) Fleet tactics at scale — multi-ship engagements with attention to formation and command hierarchy; (3) The last stand / siege arc in books 2–3; (4) Franchise evolution via new POV entry point (*Scorpio*, Kloos's Frontlines: Evolution); (5) The morally compromised mission that forces the protagonist to choose
- **Theme tropes**: (1) Military service as class escape / upward mobility; (2) The corrupted institution placing soldiers in impossible ethical positions; (3) Technology as both enabler and dehumanizer — powered armor, AI copilots, drone warfare; (4) What you owe the people beside you versus what you owe the institution above you
- **Tonal range**: KU/indie pole is action-forward, high body count, minimal literary ambition. Trad pole (Kloos, Campbell) combines action with character interiority and ethical commentary. Grimdark variant: high casualties, morally ambiguous orders, units decimated.
- **Adjacent niches**: Space opera (shares fleet combat; diverges on protagonist orientation), Near-future techno-thriller (shares action-pace; techno-thriller is systemic rather than squad-level), LitRPG military variant (power-scaling in RPG skin; audience overlap)
- **Key divergence points**: Space opera readers in Martine mode are warned away from mil-SF expectations. LitRPG military variant is stat-progression fantasy, not institution-grounded drama. Near-future techno-thriller has civilian/institutional protagonists; mil-SF is unit-embedded.
- **Recent market examples**: *Scorpio* (Frontlines: Evolution #1, Marko Kloos, 47North, 2024) — Amazon's highest-rated mil-SF 2024; *Descent* (Palladium Wars #4, Kloos, 2024); *Corvvis* (Frontlines: Evolution #2, Kloos, 2025); *The Crypt: Shakedown* (Scott Sigler, 2025) — "Best Military Sci Fi in Ages" per reader; Glynn Stewart KU catalog (Chimera's Fall, 2025)
- **Demand vs. supply assessment**: KU/indie satisfies mass-market demand; trad publishers structurally under-invest. 47North is the only meaningful trad-adjacent publisher serving this audience at scale.
