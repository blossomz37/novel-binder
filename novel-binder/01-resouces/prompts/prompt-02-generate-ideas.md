## Generate High-Concept Story Ideas

Using the contemporary science fiction market research above, generate high-concept story ideas with strong commercial positioning and clear narrative engines.

Goal: identify story premises that combine:
- a combustible story engine
- emotionally specific stakes
- physical danger and escalating external pressure
- protagonists with agency who must suffer meaningful costs
- a near-impossible goal
- an antagonist or opposing force that stays one step ahead
- wrong choices that can harm someone close to the protagonist and threaten the world as they know it

Rules:
- Ground every idea in one or more contemporary sci-fi niches from the research.
- Prioritize underserved or high-opportunity niches, but use durable market mainstays where helpful.
- Avoid passive protagonists, vague dystopias, generic rebellions, and “save the world” stakes without personal cost.
- Make each protagonist’s goal concrete, urgent, and difficult.
- Make the antagonist’s advantage specific.
- Include both emotional stakes and physical/world stakes.
- Tag all claims or market logic using the same evidence tags from the research: [SRC], [RECENT], [SALES], [READER], [PUBLISHER], [AWARD], [INFER], [LOW_CONF], [GAP].
- Mark creative invention as [IDEA], not fact.


Output 20 story concepts in this format:

1. Title Hook
   - Niche: (comma-separated list)
   - Market Logic: (evidence-tagged market fit statement)
   - Premise: (core premise, 1–2 sentences)
   - Protagonist: (brief description)
   - Goal: (concrete, urgent, difficult)
   - Antagonist: (brief description)
   - Antagonist Advantage: (specific advantage)
   - Emotional Stakes: (what’s at risk emotionally)
   - Physical/World Stakes: (what’s at risk in the world)
   - Forced Bad Choices:
     1. (first dilemma)
     2. (second dilemma)
   - Story Engine: (what keeps generating conflict scene after scene)
   - Escalation: (how pressure worsens across the story)
   - Freshness: (why this isn’t just a familiar trope repeat)
   - Comparable: (comparable titles/media)
   - Risk: (execution or market risks)
   - Tags: (evidence tags)

...repeat for all 20 ideas.

After the list, rank the top 5 ideas by:
1. commercial clarity
2. emotional intensity
3. originality within market expectations
4. series potential
5. strength of antagonist pressure
6. underserved-market fit

Output the rankings as a simple numbered list, e.g.:

## Top 5 Ideas (Ranked)
1. [Title] — [brief rationale]
2. ...

Keep output structured, specific, and decision-useful. No broad creative-writing advice. Do not output a table or JSON block—use only the nested numbered list format above.
