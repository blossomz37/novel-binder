### 2.5 Dystopian / Post-Collapse

- **Core reader expectation**: Genre resurging 2024–2026 after mid-2010s glut. Readers seek safe vicarious experience of societal breakdown — "emotional relief and new perspectives." Gender divide in aesthetics: survival-Darwinism (predominantly male authors) vs. community-building cooperation (predominantly female authors). Both audiences are active. [SRC] [READER] [Classics of SF blog](https://classicsofsciencefiction.com/2024/08/)
- **Character tropes**: (1) The ordinary survivor with no special skills who learns community formation from scratch; (2) The resistant holdout who refuses new regime's norms; (3) The hidden truth-teller who holds pre-collapse knowledge (Haymitch in *Sunrise on the Reaping*); (4) The reluctant rebel whose humanity is the resistance; (5) The complicit insider who finally acts
- **Plot tropes**: (1) The slow revelation of how the world ended — flashback while present survival continues; (2) The false refuge — seeming-safe community with controlling ideology or hidden horror; (3) The resistance movement vs. totalitarian enforcement (now hybridized with romance elements — forced mating contract in *Conform*, 2025); (4) The competition-as-control system that generates the plot; (5) The prequel that expands political worldbuilding (*Sunrise on the Reaping*)
- **Theme tropes**: (1) The surveillance state and algorithmically controlled citizen (*In the Algorithm We Trust*, Ryan David Ginsberg, 2025: "1984 for the data-mined generation"); (2) Class warfare in disaster — cyborg billionaires partying while middle class collapses (*Local Heavens*); (3) AI as new authority — heavy in 2025 pipeline; (4) Political manipulation and propaganda; (5) The enclosure of the commons — who owns public resources
- **Tonal range**: YA/crossover (Collins — accessible, romance-inflected, resistance-forward); literary adult (Ling Ma, Mandel — prose-forward, melancholic, ambiguous); dark/grimdark (full nihilism, system never falls)
- **Adjacent niches**: Cli-fi (shares post-collapse scaffolding; dystopia is cause-agnostic); YA SF (YA dystopia is the dominant YA SF form); SFR (dystopian romance hybrid growing 2025)
- **Key divergence points**: YA dystopia has faster pacing, clearer moral resolution, identity formation as central theme. Adult dystopia can be fully unresolved. Cli-fi is cause-specific; dystopia can arise from AI, plague, politics. Dystopian-romance hybrid (*Conform*) is a distinct and growing variant.
- **Recent market examples**: *Sunrise on the Reaping* (Suzanne Collins, Scholastic, March 2025) — 1.5M copies first week globally; *You Weren't Meant to be Human* (Andrew Joseph White, 2025); *A Hole in the Sky* (Peter F. Hamilton, Jan 2026); *Event Horizon* (Balsam Karam, March 2026); *A Fate So Cold* (Chloe Gong, Tor Teen, Nov 2025)
- **Demand vs. supply assessment**: YA dystopia is explicitly back; adult dystopia is "getting more diverse, sharper, and more relevant by the year."
