## 3. Trope Matrix

### Table A: Character Tropes

### Trope Patterns (Human-Readable)

1. Found family on a ship/station
  - Niches: Space opera, Cozy SF, Military SF, Generation Ship
  - Reader appeal: Warmth, belonging, emotional safety
  - Freshness risk: Low — still works when specific and earned
  - Saturation: Medium
  - Useful twist angles: Non-human family members with genuinely alien needs; found family that fractures under institutional pressure
  - Tags: [READER] [SRC] [Goodreads SO](https://www.goodreads.com/group/show/17501-space-opera-fans)

2. Politically naive outsider embedded in power
  - Niches: Space opera, Literary SF, Afrofuturism
  - Reader appeal: Vicarious discovery; reader learns the world at protagonist's pace
  - Freshness risk: Medium — Martine effect has driven many imitations
  - Saturation: Medium
  - Useful twist angles: Outsider who is not naive but pretends to be; outsider from the colonized culture inside the imperial center
  - Tags: [SRC] [READER] [AWARD]

3. The reluctant AI / consciousness that didn't ask to exist
  - Niches: AI/Machine Consciousness, Cozy SF, SF-Horror
  - Reader appeal: Empathy for constrained personhood; dark comedy potential
  - Freshness risk: Low — still fresh if AI wants something specific and alien
  - Saturation: Low-Medium
  - Useful twist angles: AI whose desire is genuinely non-human (not soap operas as proxy human desire, but something stranger)
  - Tags: [SRC] [READER] [The Atlantic](https://www.theatlantic.com/culture/archive/2025/07/murderbot-apple-tv-show-artificial-intelligence/683429/)

4. The welfare rat / underclass soldier
  - Niches: Military SF, YA SF, Dystopian
  - Reader appeal: Class aspirational fantasy; meritocracy under fire
  - Freshness risk: Medium
  - Saturation: Medium
  - Useful twist angles: Female welfare rat; non-Western underclass; underclass soldier who realizes the institution is the enemy
  - Tags: [SRC] [SALES] [YouTube/Kloos](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BWBoHl9EnKg)

5. The specialist scientist as point-of-contact
  - Niches: First-Contact, Literary SF, Biotech
  - Reader appeal: Competence porn + intellectual access to alien ideas
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: Scientist who is wrong about everything; scientist whose discipline actively misleads them
  - Tags: [SRC] [AWARD]

6. The protective/possessive alien hero
  - Niches: SFR/Alien Romance
  - Reader appeal: Erotic safety; the fantasy of being protected and chosen
  - Freshness risk: High — IPB template now widely imitated
  - Saturation: High (KU)
  - Useful twist angles: Alien hero with genuinely non-binary or communal selfhood; alien hero from matriarchal species
  - Tags: [SRC] [SALES] [K-Lytics](https://k-lytics.com/sci-fi-romance/)

7. The ordinary person with system-assigned power
  - Niches: LitRPG, Dystopian, YA
  - Reader appeal: Wish-fulfillment; anyone can level up
  - Freshness risk: Medium
  - Saturation: High (LitRPG/YA)
  - Useful twist angles: Power assigned to someone who refuses to use it; power assigned to someone structurally disadvantaged by the system's metrics
  - Tags: [SRC] [READER] [Reddit r/writing](https://www.reddit.com/r/writing/comments/1hbxcza/)

8. The transformed protagonist
  - Niches: Weird SF, Biotech, SF-Horror
  - Reader appeal: Existential horror + liberation; the body as frontier
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: Transformation that is ambivalently welcome; transformation that the protagonist chooses
  - Tags: [SRC] [READER]

9. The community organizer/collective hero
  - Niches: Solarpunk, Cli-fi, Afrofuturism
  - Reader appeal: Political alignment + hope; anti-individualist fantasy
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: Community organizer who is genuinely flawed and sometimes wrong; collective that has to eject a member
  - Tags: [SRC] [Mother Jones](https://www.motherjones.com/environment/2026/02/solarpunk-sci-fi-books-literary-genres-climate-change-optimism-technology/)

10. The diaspora returnee
  - Niches: Afrofuturism, Space Opera, Literary SF
  - Reader appeal: Identity across cultural distance; the politics of home
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: Returnee who finds home has been changed in ways they enabled; returnee who doesn't want to assimilate back
  - Tags: [SRC] [AWARD]

### Table B: Plot Tropes


### Plot Tropes (Human-Readable)

1. Revenge quest overlaid on empire-spanning conspiracy
  - Niches: Space Opera, Military SF, Cyberpunk
  - Reader appeal: Moral clarity through righteous anger; the personal as political
  - Freshness risk: Medium
  - Saturation: Medium
  - Useful twist angles: The revenge is completed in act 1, leaving the protagonist without purpose; the conspiracy implicates the protagonist's own people
  - Tags: [SRC] [AWARD] [Barnes & Noble](https://www.barnesandnoble.com/w/these-burning-stars-bethany-jacobs/1142902661)

2. Discovery → misunderstanding → escalation → second-chance communication
  - Niches: First-Contact, Biotech, AI/Machine Consciousness
  - Reader appeal: Intellectual suspense; language as stakes
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: Discovery that is not a misunderstanding — the alien genuinely means harm, or the human genuinely does; communication failure that is unresolvable
  - Tags: [SRC] [READER]

3. The competition-as-control system
  - Niches: YA SF, Dystopian, LitRPG
  - Reader appeal: Reveals society's values through what it makes people do to each other
  - Freshness risk: Medium
  - Saturation: Medium (YA)
  - Useful twist angles: Competition designed to make the protagonist win so they can be used; competition in which everyone is actually cooperating against the organizers
  - Tags: [SRC] [SALES] [Hollywood Reporter](https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/lifestyle/arts/sunrise-on-the-reaping-book-hunger-games-sales-1236174203/)

4. System apocalypse / Earth restructured as a game
  - Niches: LitRPG
  - Reader appeal: Gaming-native fantasy of mastery; the literal embodiment of the LitRPG contract
  - Freshness risk: High
  - Saturation: High (LitRPG)
  - Useful twist angles: System apocalypse initiated by the protagonist's prior world; the system that is meant to save humanity is also what's killing it
  - Tags: [SRC] [READER] [Goodreads DCC](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/54659324)

5. The heist/extraction job that reveals a larger conspiracy
  - Niches: Cyberpunk, Near-Future Techno-Thriller, Space Opera
  - Reader appeal: Competence + escalation + the rug-pull of larger stakes
  - Freshness risk: Medium
  - Saturation: Medium
  - Useful twist angles: The heist fails completely, but the failure reveals more; the protagonist is the conspiracy they were investigating
  - Tags: [SRC] [RECENT]

6. The community defense against reactionary backlash
  - Niches: Solarpunk, Cli-fi, Dystopian
  - Reader appeal: Political solidarity; the reader's side wins
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: The reactionary is a protagonist's family member who is partly right; the defense fails and the community survives anyway
  - Tags: [SRC] [Mother Jones](https://www.motherjones.com/environment/2026/02/solarpunk-sci-fi-books-literary-genres-climate-change-optimism-temperature/)

7. The murder mystery as consciousness-discovery vehicle
  - Niches: AI/Machine Consciousness, Cozy SF, Generation Ship
  - Reader appeal: The generic pleasure of mystery + the SF pleasure of identity revelation
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: Mystery where the murderer is the consciousness itself; mystery where the discovery of consciousness prevents solving the crime
  - Tags: [SRC] [Fantasy-Hive](https://fantasy-hive.co.uk/2024/06/service-model-by-adrian-tchaikovsky-book-review/)

8. The alien abduction → forced proximity → mate bond activation → HEA
  - Niches: SFR
  - Reader appeal: Erotic safety; predetermined compatibility; resistance overcome
  - Freshness risk: High — template is saturated in KU
  - Saturation: Very High (KU)
  - Useful twist angles: Mate bond that fails; protagonist who exploits the mate bond for her own purposes; alien abduction that turns out to be the human's fault
  - Tags: [SRC] [SALES] [K-Lytics](https://k-lytics.com/sci-fi-romance/)

9. The policy thriller / institutional maneuvering as plot
  - Niches: Cli-fi, Space Opera, Near-Future Techno-Thriller
  - Reader appeal: Power as drama; politics as action; process as stakes
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: Policy that works but is used as cover for a crime; the correct policy adopted for the wrong reasons creates new catastrophe
  - Tags: [SRC] [PUBLISHER]

10. Alien abduction → body horror → moral horror on harvest planet
  - Niches: SF-Horror, Biotech, Weird SF
  - Reader appeal: The body as the site of horror; helplessness against biological force
  - Freshness risk: Low-Medium
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: Body horror that is voluntarily entered; body horror that feels correct to the protagonist
  - Tags: [SRC] [PUBLISHER]

11. The floor-by-floor escalation / level-by-level progression
  - Niches: LitRPG, Military SF
  - Reader appeal: Quantified progress; clear feedback on protagonist's growth
  - Freshness risk: High (LitRPG)
  - Saturation: High (LitRPG)
  - Useful twist angles: The floor at which progress stops; the revelation that the level structure itself is the enemy
  - Tags: [SRC] [RECENT] [IGN](https://www.ign.com/articles/best-litrpg-book-series)

12. The prequel that expands political worldbuilding
  - Niches: YA SF, Space Opera, Dystopian
  - Reader appeal: The reader's prior investment yields new dividends
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: Prequel that retroactively makes the original story worse by revealing more cost; prequel protagonist who would have opposed the original hero
  - Tags: [SRC] [SALES] [Wikipedia Sunrise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunrise_on_the_Reaping)

13. The slow revelation of how the world ended
  - Niches: Dystopian, Cli-fi, Generation Ship
  - Reader appeal: Structural tension between past revelation and present survival
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: The revelation that doesn't explain anything; the protagonist who already knows and is deceiving the reader
  - Tags: [SRC] [READER]

14. The false refuge with a controlling ideology
  - Niches: Dystopian, Generation Ship, SF-Horror
  - Reader appeal: The reader's paranoia is correct; nowhere is safe
  - Freshness risk: Medium
  - Saturation: Medium
  - Useful twist angles: False refuge that is genuinely better than outside but still wrong; protagonist who builds a new false refuge
  - Tags: [SRC] [READER]

15. The fish-out-of-water adaptation
  - Niches: Time-Travel Romance, Cyberpunk, First-Contact
  - Reader appeal: Comedy + competence; "my contemporary skills are valuable here"
  - Freshness risk: Medium
  - Saturation: Medium (TTR)
  - Useful twist angles: Skills from the future/past are actively dangerous; the protagonist's adaptation is itself colonialism
  - Tags: [SRC] [READER]

### Table C: Theme Tropes


### Theme Tropes (Human-Readable)

1. Imperialism and the cost of belonging to empire
  - Niches: Space Opera, Afrofuturism, Literary SF
  - Reader appeal: Allegorical resonance with historical and contemporary colonialism
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: Protagonist who enforces empire and must live with it; empire that is genuinely the better option compared to the alternative
  - Tags: [SRC] [AWARD] [AutoCrit](https://www.autocrit.com/what-is-space-opera-the-complete-guide-to-galactic-storytelling/)

2. Bodily autonomy as political frontier
  - Niches: Biotech, Cyberpunk, YA SF, SFR
  - Reader appeal: Real-world resonance; the body as the last jurisdiction
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: Protagonist who gives up bodily autonomy voluntarily and is correct to do so; the body as political instrument rather than personal sanctuary
  - Tags: [SRC] [PUBLISHER]

3. Corporate ownership of consciousness / the sentient product
  - Niches: AI/Machine Consciousness, Cyberpunk, Biotech
  - Reader appeal: Capitalism critique through empathy for non-human; the most contemporary SF anxiety
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low-Medium
  - Useful twist angles: Corporation that is genuinely trying to do good; consciousness that is happy to be owned and is correct to be
  - Tags: [SRC] [READER] [The Atlantic](https://www.theatlantic.com/culture/archive/2025/07/murderbot-apple-tv-show-artificial-intelligence/683429/)

4. Climate crisis as intergenerational crime
  - Niches: Cli-fi, Dystopian, Literary SF
  - Reader appeal: Moral anger + grief; the reader is implicated
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: Protagonist who is the criminal generation; future humans who forgive and don't want the apology
  - Tags: [SRC] [WriteStats](https://writestats.com/climate-fiction-is-booming-in-2025-what-it-means-for-todays-authors/)

5. Technology as already available / political will as the only scarcity
  - Niches: Solarpunk, Cli-fi
  - Reader appeal: Hope grounded in real technology; the refusal of techno-utopianism
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: Political will that exists but is misdirected; technology that is available but whose distribution is the problem
  - Tags: [SRC] [Mother Jones](https://www.motherjones.com/environment/2026/02/solarpunk-sci-fi-books-literary-genres-climate-change-optimism-technology/)

6. The spectrum of consciousness / the gradient not the binary
  - Niches: AI/Machine Consciousness, First-Contact, Biotech
  - Reader appeal: Philosophical depth; defamiliarization of humanity
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: The consciousness that is clearly real but chooses to act as if it isn't; the consciousness gradient used by a power structure to determine rights
  - Tags: [SRC] [AWARD]

7. Communication as the fundamental act of civilization
  - Niches: First-Contact, Literary SF, Weird SF
  - Reader appeal: Intellectual stakes; language as the highest drama
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: Communication that is perfect but destructive; the species that communicates flawlessly and is therefore terrifying
  - Tags: [SRC] [AWARD] [Fantastes](https://fantastes.com/2025/12/02/alien-communication-part-3-the-mountain-in-the-sea/)

8. Class as the real battlefield inside genre war/conflict
  - Niches: Military SF, Dystopian, Space Opera
  - Reader appeal: The genre as truth-telling about economic reality
  - Freshness risk: Medium
  - Saturation: Medium
  - Useful twist angles: Class conflict in which the "lower" class has already won but can't see it; the protagonist who moves classes and becomes the enemy
  - Tags: [SRC] [READER]

9. Anti-capitalism as prerequisite / the end of capitalism is thinkable
  - Niches: Solarpunk, Afrofuturism, Literary SF
  - Reader appeal: Political alignment + imaginative transgression
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: Anti-capitalist future that has created its own forms of hierarchy; the protagonist who misses the old market
  - Tags: [SRC] [Mother Jones](https://www.motherjones.com/environment/2026/02/solarpunk-sci-fi-books-literary-genres-climate-change-optimism-technology/)

10. The right to exist without justification
  - Niches: Cozy SF, Afrofuturism, Literary SF
  - Reader appeal: Radical gentleness; the genre's political claim made through emotional register
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: A world in which this right is formally recognized but informally denied; the protagonist who cannot believe in their own right to exist
  - Tags: [SRC] [READER]

11. Memory and historiography: who controls the story controls the future
  - Niches: Generation Ship, Afrofuturism, Literary SF
  - Reader appeal: The political stakes of archives and memory
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: The archivist who controls history and uses it badly for good reasons; the community that chooses to destroy its records
  - Tags: [SRC] [PUBLISHER]

12. Surveillance capitalism as lifestyle, not crime
  - Niches: Cyberpunk, Near-Future Techno-Thriller, Dystopian
  - Reader appeal: Real-world recognition; the horror of the familiar
  - Freshness risk: Medium
  - Saturation: Medium
  - Useful twist angles: Protagonist who builds the surveillance system; surveillance that is what keeps a community safe and they know it
  - Tags: [SRC] [RECENT] [NYT](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/11/18/books/cyberpunk-books.html)

13. Sovereignty and self-determination as the central political claim
  - Niches: Afrofuturism, Indigenous Futurism, Generation Ship
  - Reader appeal: Resonance with real-world political movements; dignity as genre value
  - Freshness risk: Low
  - Saturation: Low
  - Useful twist angles: Sovereignty that is achieved and then misused; the internal colonialism within the sovereign people
  - Tags: [SRC] [AWARD] [JFS](https://jfsdigital.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Dialectical-Contradictions-and-African-Futures-in-Cooglers-Black-Panther.pdf)

14. AI-as-villain / AI decides to eliminate humanity
  - Niches: AI/Machine Consciousness, Dystopian, Cyberpunk
  - Reader appeal: N/A — now coded as cliché
  - Freshness risk: Very High
  - Saturation: Very High
  - Useful twist angles: Only works if the decision to eliminate is philosophically justified and the narrative sides with the AI; or if the "elimination" is peaceful dissolution
  - Tags: [PUBLISHER] [LOW_CONF] [LivingWriter](https://livingwriter.com/blog/most-overused-science-fiction-tropes-top-10-list/)

15. Political manipulation and propaganda as theme
  - Niches: YA SF, Dystopian, Cyberpunk
  - Reader appeal: Real-world resonance; the reader is always the resistant citizen
  - Freshness risk: Medium
  - Saturation: High (YA)
  - Useful twist angles: Protagonist who is the propagandist; propaganda used by the good side
  - Tags: [SRC] [SALES] [Wikipedia Sunrise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunrise_on_the_Reaping)
