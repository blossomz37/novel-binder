## 5. Mainstays


### Mainstays

1. Murderbot-style snarky AI with dark comedy
  - Why it persists: Empathy for constrained personhood; the snark is defensive, not cynical; introversion as reader identification
  - Current examples: *Service Model* (Tchaikovsky, 2024); *Murderbot* TV series (Apple TV+, 2025)
  - How to use without feeling stale: Give the AI something it wants that is genuinely non-human and strange; don't just use snark as personality substitute for depth
  - Tags: [SRC] [READER] [AWARD] [The Atlantic](https://www.theatlantic.com/culture/archive/2025/07/murderbot-apple-tv-show-artificial-intelligence/683429/)

2. Found family on a ship
  - Why it persists: Warmth, belonging, the feeling that the crew would die for each other; transferable to any SF setting
  - Current examples: *The Long Way to a Small, Angry Planet* (Chambers); *Gravity Lost* (L.M. Sagas, Tor, 2024)
  - How to use without feeling stale: Make the family genuinely chosen under duress; show what it costs; give at least one member an irreconcilable difference
  - Tags: [SRC] [READER] [PUBLISHER] [Tor/Forge](https://www.torforgeblog.com/2024/01/07/our-ongoing-series-of-2024/)

3. The competence-porn first-contact protagonist
  - Why it persists: Satisfaction of expertise applied under impossible pressure; scientific method as heroism
  - Current examples: *The Mountain in the Sea* (Nayler); *Alien Clay* (Tchaikovsky, 2024); Hugo-nominated work pattern
  - How to use without feeling stale: Let the expertise fail at least once in a consequential way; the protagonist's disciplinary assumptions must be challenged, not just their limits
  - Tags: [SRC] [AWARD] [READER]

4. The resistance protagonist in a surveillance dystopia
  - Why it persists: Political catharsis; being right when everyone is wrong; the individual vs. the state
  - Current examples: *Sunrise on the Reaping* (Collins, 2025); *You Weren't Meant to be Human* (White, 2025)
  - How to use without feeling stale: The resistance must cost something that matters; the protagonist cannot win cleanly; the surveillance must be specific to 2025 tech, not generic Big Brother
  - Tags: [SRC] [SALES] [RECENT] [Hollywood Reporter](https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/lifestyle/arts/sunrise-on-the-reaping-book-hunger-games-sales-1236174203/)

5. The trilogy closer that makes everything matter
  - Why it persists: Reader investment in prior books is rewarded; the series ending as cultural event
  - Current examples: *Onyx Storm* (Yarros, 2025 — 8 weeks at NYT #1); *Children of Strife* (Tchaikovsky, 2025/2026)
  - How to use without feeling stale: The ending must be earned by the preceding character work, not by escalating plot stakes alone; character resolution > plot resolution
  - Tags: [SRC] [SALES] [RECENT] [NYT Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_The_New_York_Times_number-one_books_of_2025)

6. The empire built on an atrocity that must be reckoned with
  - Why it persists: Historical/colonial resonance; moral seriousness that genre can carry; the past is never past
  - Current examples: *These Burning Stars* (Jacobs, Philip K. Dick Award 2024); *A Memory Called Empire* (Martine)
  - How to use without feeling stale: Don't use the atrocity as backstory texture — it must be structurally central; the protagonist must be complicit, not just a witness
  - Tags: [SRC] [AWARD] [RECENT] [Barnes & Noble](https://www.barnesandnoble.com/w/these-burning-stars-bethany-jacobs/1142902661)

7. The alien species as philosophical argument
  - Why it persists: SF's unique capability — the alien externalizes an abstract question about humanity
  - Current examples: *Children of Time*'s spiders (Tchaikovsky); *Translation State*'s Presger (Leckie)
  - How to use without feeling stale: The alien species must want something coherent that they'd want if they were not human; no telepathy-as-shortcut; their logic must be internally consistent
  - Tags: [SRC] [READER] [Reddit r/printSF](https://www.reddit.com/r/printSF/comments/1qr8vzn/)

8. The upmarket literary premise (one idea, philosophical rigor)
  - Why it persists: Critical prestige; mainstream review coverage; the reader wants to be stretched; Ted Chiang template
  - Current examples: *The Ministry of Time* (Bradley, 2024 — #1 reader pick); *Hum* (Phillips, 2024); *Death of the Author* (Okorafor, 2025)
  - How to use without feeling stale: The idea must be genuinely new or approached from a genuinely unexpected angle; "what if X were true" is not enough — must be "what would we have to give up if X were true"
  - Tags: [SRC] [AWARD] [SALES] [Reddit r/printSF](https://www.reddit.com/r/printSF/comments/1hrra20/)

9. Fated mates / mate bond mechanics
  - Why it persists: The romance reader's contract: chemistry as fate, safety through predetermination, resistance overcome
  - Current examples: Ruby Dixon IPB (ongoing KU); *Bride* (Hazelwood, 2024)
  - How to use without feeling stale: To avoid feeling like a template-fill, the mate bond must impose genuine cost or complication beyond the initial resistance; the fated element should surprise the protagonist in a specific way
  - Tags: [SRC] [SALES] [READER] [K-Lytics](https://k-lytics.com/sci-fi-romance/)

10. The post-apocalyptic community formation
  - Why it persists: Humans rebuilding society is inherently dramatic; the reader imagines themselves in the group
  - Current examples: *The Light Pirate* (Brooks-Dalton); *A Psalm for the Wild-Built* (Chambers); *The Lost Cause* (Doctorow)
  - How to use without feeling stale: The community must fail at something; show the governance fights, the expulsions, the decisions that haunt; don't make it utopian
  - Tags: [SRC] [READER] [PUBLISHER]

11. The protagonist who rises through ranks under institutional pressure
  - Why it persists: Class mobility + meritocracy + institutional critique; the underdog story with SF amplification
  - Current examples: Kloos's Frontlines series (Scorpio, 2024); Dungeon Crawler Carl
  - How to use without feeling stale: Give the institution a structural flaw that the protagonist must either fix or break; the rank achievement must cost something the protagonist valued before they started
  - Tags: [SRC] [SALES] [RECENT] [YouTube/Kloos](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BWBoHl9EnKg)

12. Horror of the trapped crew / isolation with a threat
  - Why it persists: Visceral fear + the intimacy of a small group under extreme pressure; Alien template still works
  - Current examples: *Cold Eternity* (S.A. Barnes, Tor Nightfire, 2025); *The Library at Hellebore* (Khaw, 2025)
  - How to use without feeling stale: The threat must be specific, not generic; the crew dynamic must generate conflict independent of the threat; someone must make a choice that condemns the others
  - Tags: [SRC] [PUBLISHER] [RECENT] [Grimdark Magazine](https://www.grimdarkmagazine.com/the-dark-fantasy-sf-and-horror-novels-we-cant-wait-to-read-in-2025/)
